KEY POINTS FROM ARTICLE

Procurement AI attracted $2.1B in VC funding in Q1 2026 alone — more than the previous 2 years combined.
The explosion is being driven by enterprises discovering that procurement is a "data-rich, decision-poor"
function: companies have enormous data on suppliers, contracts, and spend, but have been unable to use it.

TOP FUNDED COMPANIES (Q1 2026):
- Pactum AI ($180M Series C) — autonomous negotiation for supplier contracts
- Zip ($150M Series D) — intake-to-procure workflow automation
- Jagger ($95M Series B) — supplier risk intelligence using real-time news and financial signals
- Odin Procurement ($72M Series B) — tail spend analytics and automation for mid-market

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE OBSERVATIONS:
- Legacy players (SAP Ariba, Oracle Procurement, Coupa) are integrating AI via acquisitions
- Platform wars emerging: will procurement AI be a standalone category or absorbed into ERP?
- Microsoft Copilot for Finance now includes procurement workflows — threatens pure-play startups

INTERESTING QUOTE from Pactum CEO:
"The average Fortune 500 has 45,000 supplier contracts. Their legal team reviews 2,000 of them per year.
The other 43,000 just auto-renew on whatever terms the supplier set 5 years ago. That's the problem we solve."

CONTRARIAN TAKE POTENTIAL:
The funding boom may be creating a procurement AI bubble. The actual ROI evidence is thin —
most case studies are vendor-supplied with no independent verification. The McKinsey survey
(see article2) is the most credible data but even that is self-reported by buyers.
