KEY POINTS FROM ARTICLE

The $13 trillion global procurement market is experiencing its most significant technology shift since ERP
systems in the 1990s. AI is not just automating procurement tasks — it's changing who makes procurement
decisions and how.

Three transformations underway:

1. SUPPLIER DISCOVERY AND RISK SCORING
Companies like Coupa, SAP Ariba, and a wave of startups are using LLMs trained on supplier databases,
news feeds, and financial records to surface supplier risk in real time. Buyers at companies like Unilever
and Boeing now see supplier financial health scores updated daily rather than annually.

Before AI: procurement teams ran annual supplier audits. Typical risk review: 3-6 months lead time.
After AI: continuous monitoring; high-risk suppliers flagged within 48 hours of deteriorating signals.

2. CONTRACT INTELLIGENCE
Legal teams are using AI to analyse thousands of supplier contracts simultaneously, identifying
non-standard clauses, expiring terms, and untapped volume discount triggers. One Fortune 500 CPO
quoted in the article: "We found £4.2M in unused contracted discounts in our first AI contract audit."

3. DEMAND FORECASTING FOR TAIL SPEND
Tail spend (purchases outside preferred suppliers) typically represents 20% of procurement value
but 80% of procurement transactions. AI can now forecast tail spend patterns and proactively suggest
preferred alternatives — reducing maverick spend by 30-40% in early adopters.

CONTRARIAN TAKE POTENTIAL:
The article is bullish on AI procurement. A contrarian angle: AI procurement optimisation favours
incumbent large suppliers who have data partnerships with platforms. SME suppliers are being
systematically disadvantaged — an unintended consequence worth examining.
