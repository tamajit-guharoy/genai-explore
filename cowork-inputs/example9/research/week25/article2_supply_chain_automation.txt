KEY POINTS FROM ARTICLE

McKinsey surveyed 400 supply chain leaders across manufacturing, retail, and logistics.
Key finding: 43% have deployed AI in at least one supply chain function; only 8% have achieved
"autonomous" operation in any process. The gap between pilots and scale is the central problem.

THREE TIERS OF MATURITY IDENTIFIED:

Tier 1 — Optimised (43% of respondents):
AI used for demand forecasting and inventory optimisation. Humans still approve all decisions.
Typical ROI: 5-12% cost reduction.

Tier 2 — Augmented (34% of respondents):
AI handles routine procurement decisions autonomously (routine POs under threshold, reorder triggers).
Human oversight for exceptions only.
Typical ROI: 15-25% cost reduction, 30% reduction in procurement headcount for routine work.

Tier 3 — Autonomous (8% of respondents):
AI makes supplier selection, contract negotiation (within parameters), and payment decisions.
Humans set policy and manage exceptions.
Typical ROI: 35%+ cost reduction.

BARRIERS TO SCALING (from survey):
1. Data quality — fragmented supplier master data prevents AI from reasoning across contracts (67%)
2. Change management — procurement teams fear displacement (54%)
3. Legal/compliance — organisations uncertain about liability for AI-made procurement decisions (48%)
4. Integration — AI tools don't connect to legacy ERP systems without expensive custom work (71%)

STAT FOR THE ARTICLE:
"By 2028, McKinsey estimates 25% of all B2B procurement transactions by value will involve
no human decision point between supplier selection and purchase order issuance."

CONTRARIAN TAKE POTENTIAL:
The "autonomous" tier in this report is largely confined to commodity goods and services.
For complex, strategic procurement (consulting, technology, capital equipment), AI is nowhere
near autonomous decision-making. McKinsey's headline number is doing a lot of work on a narrow slice.
