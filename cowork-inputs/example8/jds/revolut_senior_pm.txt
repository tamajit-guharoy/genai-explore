JOB DESCRIPTION

Role: Senior Product Manager — Business Payments
Company: Revolut
Location: London, UK (Hybrid)
Team: Business Product
Reports to: Head of Product, Business

---

ABOUT REVOLUT
Revolut is a global fintech company with 45 million customers worldwide. We're on a mission to deliver
10x better financial services. We're regulated in 35+ countries and process $500B+ in transactions annually.

---

THE ROLE
We're looking for a Senior PM to own Business Payments — the products that allow our 2.5 million business
customers to send money, manage multi-currency accounts, and automate their financial operations.

You'll be working on one of Revolut's fastest-growing product areas, with a high degree of autonomy
and a strong emphasis on moving fast and proving impact through data.

---

RESPONSIBILITIES
- Own the product strategy for business payments (transfers, bulk payments, FX, payroll integrations)
- Define, build, and launch features used by 2.5M business customers across 35+ markets
- Lead discovery: deep customer research with SMBs and enterprise clients to uncover unmet needs
- Work closely with engineering, design, data, compliance, and operations teams
- Define success metrics, run A/B tests, iterate rapidly based on data
- Communicate roadmap and outcomes to executive stakeholders

---

REQUIREMENTS
Must have:
- 5+ years PM experience in fintech or B2B SaaS
- Strong payments domain knowledge: SWIFT, SEPA, Faster Payments, multi-currency
- Data-driven decision making — comfortable writing SQL queries and interpreting A/B test results
- Experience with cross-border payments or multi-currency products
- Strong communication skills — able to present to C-suite

Nice to have:
- Experience with Open Banking API products
- Knowledge of SMB or enterprise financial needs
- Multi-market product launches

---

REVOLUT'S PRINCIPLES
Think bigger. Build better. Deliver faster. We value candidates who challenge assumptions and
thrive in ambiguity. Don't expect a gentle pace — we move fast, iterate constantly, and celebrate
both successes and well-reasoned failures.

---

COMPENSATION
£110,000–£135,000 + bonus + equity
