Title: Paired TMS-BCI Stimulation Enhances Cortical Plasticity Compared to BCI Alone in Chronic Stroke: A Pilot RCT
Authors: DeLuca, M., Fischer, W., Yamamoto, T., Chen, W.
Journal: Neurorehabilitation and Neural Repair, 2025
DOI: 10.1177/15459683251089442

HYPOTHESIS
Paired transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) delivered in synchrony with BCI-triggered motor
attempts will induce greater cortical plasticity (indexed by TMS motor evoked potentials) than BCI alone.

METHODOLOGY
Pilot RCT. 24 chronic stroke survivors (>6 months post-stroke).
Randomised to: (A) BCI + paired TMS (n=12), (B) BCI alone (n=12).
Intervention: 15 sessions over 3 weeks.
Primary outcome: MEP amplitude change from cortical motor map.
Secondary outcomes: Fugl-Meyer Assessment, grip strength.

KEY RESULTS
- MEP amplitude change: Group A +42% vs Group B +18% (p=0.031)
- Fugl-Meyer (upper extremity): Group A +7.2 points vs Group B +4.8 points (p=0.089 — trend only)
- Grip strength: no significant difference
- No serious adverse events in TMS group; 2 mild headaches

LIMITATIONS
- Small sample (pilot study) — underpowered for Fugl-Meyer; results are hypothesis-generating only
- TMS is expensive equipment not available in most rehabilitation settings — limits translation
- TMS operator skill may confound results (not blinded)

RELEVANCE TO: Neural interfaces in motor rehabilitation — MEDIUM-HIGH
Supports the general direction of closed-loop stimulation but TMS is not scalable. FES remains
more practical. Useful for introduction framing and contrasting stimulation modalities.
