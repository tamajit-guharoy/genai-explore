SECTION 1: VISION (DRAFT — needs revision)

The challenge of restoring motor function after stroke affects 13 million people annually worldwide.
Despite decades of research, recovery outcomes remain poor and inconsistent. The central hypothesis
of this fellowship is that personalised, closed-loop brain-computer interface (BCI) therapy — adapting
stimulation parameters to the individual's real-time neural state — will outperform fixed-protocol
rehabilitation by 40% on standard outcome measures.
