Dr. Alex Morgan is a Senior Research Fellow in Neuroscience at the University of Bristol,
where they lead the Neural Interfaces and Motor Rehabilitation Group. Their research focuses
on EEG-based brain-computer interfaces for post-stroke motor recovery, combining real-time
neural signal processing with functional electrical stimulation to promote cortical plasticity.

Dr. Morgan holds a PhD from the University of Oxford and was previously a postdoctoral
researcher at ETH Zurich. They are Principal Investigator on a UKRI Future Leaders Fellowship
(£1.2M) and co-lead a NIHR-funded clinical trial in collaboration with Royal Bristol Hospital.
Their 2026 randomised controlled trial (J NeuroEng Rehabil) demonstrated 34% greater upper
limb recovery with BCI-assisted therapy compared to conventional treatment.

Dr. Morgan is an Associate Editor at the Journal of NeuroEngineering and Rehabilitation
and has delivered invited talks at IEEE EMBC and the British Neuroscience Association meeting.
