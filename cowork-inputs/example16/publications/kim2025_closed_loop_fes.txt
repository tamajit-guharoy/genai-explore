Title: Closed-Loop Functional Electrical Stimulation Driven by EEG Motor Imagery: A Feasibility Study
Authors: Kim, J., Morgan, A., Fischer, W.
Journal: IEEE Transactions on Neural Systems and Rehabilitation Engineering
Volume: 33, Pages 1841–1850
Date: November 2025
DOI: 10.1109/TNSRE.2025.3489012
Type: Original Research Article

ABSTRACT
Functional electrical stimulation (FES) combined with motor imagery can promote cortical plasticity.
We describe a closed-loop system in which EEG-decoded motor imagery of wrist extension triggers
immediate FES delivery to the affected limb. In a 20-participant feasibility study (subacute stroke),
the closed-loop group showed significantly greater cortical reorganisation (fMRI neuroimaging) and
faster response latency on voluntary movement tasks compared to open-loop FES control. System latency
(EEG decode to stimulation delivery) was 187ms — within therapeutic window. This feasibility study
provides the basis for the larger RCT reported in Chen et al. 2026.

KEY CONTRIBUTION: Technical validation of the closed-loop EEG-FES system design; establishes feasibility
for the Phase III trial.
