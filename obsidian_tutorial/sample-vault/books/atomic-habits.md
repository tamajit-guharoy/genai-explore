# Atomic Habits

> **Author**: James Clear | **Genre**: Self-Help | **Rating**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
> **Status**: Reading | **Started**: May 10, 2026
> [[Deep Work]] | [[The Power of Habit]]

## Why I'm Reading This

Want to build better systems for my daily workflow. Especially interested in the "habit stacking" and "environment design" concepts for my morning routine and coding sessions.

## Key Ideas

### 1. The 1% Rule
Small improvements compound. Getting 1% better each day = 37x better in a year.

### 2. The Four Laws of Behavior Change

```
Make it Obvious   → Cue
Make it Attractive → Craving
Make it Easy      → Response
Make it Satisfying → Reward
```

### 3. Identity-Based Habits

> "The goal is not to read a book. The goal is to become a reader."

Focus on who you want to become, not what you want to achieve.

### 4. Habit Stacking

Formula: "After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]"
Example: "After I pour my morning coffee, I will meditate for one minute."

## Quotes

> "You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems."

> "Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you wish to become."
